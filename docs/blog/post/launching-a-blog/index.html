Launching a blog

Recently I experienced something one might describe as a kind of epiphany, although I'm quite embarassed to not think that way about it earlier.

I had been playing around with different programming languages, building small just-for-fun projects for about 20 years and professionally developing software for just over a decade when I figured that I might have collected one or two pieces of knowledge along the way that might be worth sharing and was searching for ways to do so.
As a first step I decided to intensify my contributions to open source.

Along that way I stumbled over the Testing Library repositories. Kent has done a phenomenal job there, bringing people from different backgrounds together and creating a welcoming environment for new contributors. He does the same at his Discord. So I went with it and answered some questions that came up in the chat over the last couple of weeks.

Then it hit me:

When working on codebases I've been an advocate of DRY principle for a very long time.
And yet I just typed a (nearly) identical response into the Discord chat for the third(?) time.

So I made a promise to myself:
When I write a response longer than 2-3 lines, I'll try to preserve the core of it as a blog post so that I later might just refer to it.

That's where we're at now. The infrastructure is set and the next post hopefully will be about some interesting coding problem.
